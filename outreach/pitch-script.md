# Pitch Script & Talking Points

## Opening (10 seconds)
"Hi, I'm Dylan — I build websites for local Boise businesses. I actually already built a free sample site for [Business Name] so you can see exactly what it'd look like. Got 60 seconds?"

## Show the site
- Pull up their page on your phone (link in `site-links.md`).
- Let them scroll it themselves if possible — people trust what they can touch more than what you describe.
- Point out one specific thing you tailored to them (their hours, a menu item, their neighborhood) — this shows it's not a generic template.

## Key talking points
- "This is already built and live — there's no risk in looking at it."
- "Right now, if someone Googles [Business Name], what do they find?" (Most of these 18 have little/no real online presence — that's why they made the shortlist.)
- "A real site like this helps customers find your hours, menu/services, and contact info without calling or guessing."
- "I can have this live on your own domain within a few days."

## Pricing & Payment
- **Setup fee: $250-300, cash, due when the site goes live** — same day if possible.
- **Monthly fee: $100/month** for hosting, keeping it live, and ongoing updates.
- Take the first month's $100 in cash along with the setup fee if they're ready to commit — makes the first payment simple: $350-400 total on the spot.
- For month 2 onward, get them set up on Venmo/Cash App/PayPal so you're not chasing cash from 18 businesses every month.
- **Always give a written/text receipt** for any cash taken — protects both of you and keeps your records straight.

## Getting their site details (after they say yes)
- Ask directly: "What would you like changed on here — hours, menu, photos, contact info, anything else?"
- Keep the ask focused: real business info (hours, phone, address, menu/services, a couple photos) is in scope for the setup fee. Bigger asks (custom design overhaul, e-commerce, extra pages) are a conversation about additional cost, not something to say yes to on the spot.
- Write their answers down right there (notes app or paper) so you don't rely on memory later — log a summary in the tracker's Notes column too.

## Handling common pushback
- **"I don't need a website, people already know us."** → "Totally fair — but new customers driving by or searching online won't. This is about the customers you're not seeing yet."
- **"How much does this cost?"** → "$250-300 to get it live today with your real info, then $100/month after that for hosting and keeping it updated."
- **"That's too much."** → Don't just cave and drop the price on the spot — ask what feels fair, and log it in the tracker so you can decide later whether to follow up with a lower offer.
- **"I need to think about it."** → "No problem — can I grab your number and follow up in a few days?" (Log this in the tracker.)
- **"Can you change X?"** → Note it down, don't promise on-the-spot edits unless it's quick.

## Close
- If interested: get a name, phone/email, take payment, and log it as Closed in the tracker.
- If not interested: thank them, leave a card, log it in the tracker so you don't circle back too soon.

---

## Marketing Upsell — DO NOT lead with this. Only bring up after they're already a paying client.
Keep the first pitch simple (just the website). Once someone's closed and has had the site a little while, that's the moment to circle back with this — not day one.

- **Google Business Profile setup/optimization** — gets them showing up on Google Maps/local search. High value, easy sell, not really "tech" so much as digital marketing.
- **Social media posting** (Instagram/Facebook) — regular content posting on their behalf.
- **Simple local ads** (boosted posts, Google local ads) — more involved, still not coding.

Suggested framing when the time comes: "Now that your site's live, want me to also help get you showing up when people search on Google, or keep your socials active?" — offer as a separate add-on fee (e.g. +$50-100/month), not bundled into the original deal.
